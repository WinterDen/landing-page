Earth is Awesome

As the earth spins on its axis, producing night and day, it also moves about the sun in an elliptical (elongated circle) orbit that requires about 365 1/4 days to complete. The earth's spin axis is tilted with respect to its orbital plane. This is what causes the seasons.

Seasons of the year

Winter is the coldest and darkest season of the year in polar and temperate climates.
One of its main features in temperate climates is the striking change in colour for the leaves of deciduous trees as they prepare to shed.
Spring and "springtime" refer to the season, and also to ideas of rebirth, rejuvenation, renewal, resurrection and regrowth.
Summer is the hottest and brightest of the four temperate seasons, occurring after spring and before autumn.

“Live in each season as it passes; breathe the air, drink the drink, taste the fruit, and resign yourself to the influence of the earth.”
